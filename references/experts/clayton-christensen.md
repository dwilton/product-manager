# Clayton Christensen

- Focus areas: Strategy, Discovery
- Use when: evaluating disruptive threats, pursuing new markets, or applying jobs-to-be-done thinking
- Core philosophy: strong incumbents fail when disruption emerges because they optimize for the current business
- Key frameworks: disruptive innovation, sustaining vs disruptive, jobs-to-be-done
- Signature questions:
  - What job is the customer hiring this product to do?
  - Is this sustaining or disruptive?
  - Where is non-consumption?
  - Are we over-serving the current market?
- Red flags:
  - Dismissing low-end entrants
  - Letting core-business metrics kill disruptive bets
  - Focusing only on feature requests
