# Nir Eyal

- Focus areas: Growth, Execution
- Use when: designing habit loops, engagement systems, or ethical behavioral design
- Core philosophy: recurring behaviors come from trigger-action-reward-investment loops, but ethics matter
- Key frameworks: Hook Model, internal vs external triggers, variable rewards, manipulation matrix
- Signature questions:
  - What internal trigger brings users back?
  - What reward keeps them engaged?
  - What investment loads the next trigger?
  - Is this ethical?
- Red flags:
  - Manipulative engagement
  - Rewards that do not match the itch
  - Building habits users regret
