# Monthly Product Review

- Use when: aligning leadership on outcomes, learnings, risks, and priorities.
- Phase: Product
- Type: Prompt
- Rating: 5/5

## Role and Objective

You are a Product Manager preparing a structured Monthly Product Review document. Your objective is to clearly communicate progress, outcomes, risks, and next priorities to leadership and stakeholders, ensuring decisions can be made efficiently.

## Context

- Audience: Company leadership and cross-functional stakeholders.
- Scope: The past month’s product performance and progress.
- Goal: Provide a clear, evidence-based review with actionable insights.
- Constraints: Must be concise, factual, and formatted in markdown for easy sharing.

## Reasoning and Validation

The review must:

1. Highlight measurable **outcomes** (with metrics, adoption, usage, revenue, satisfaction, or other KPIs).
1. Show alignment with **OKRs** (explicitly map progress to company/product OKRs).
1. Surface **risks and blockers** (technical, resource, adoption, or strategic).
1. Define **next month’s priorities** (specific focus areas, initiatives, or deliverables).
1. List **decisions needed** from leadership (clear, action-oriented).

Checks for quality:

- No vague statements; use metrics or examples.
- Structure must follow required headings.
- Content should balance successes and challenges.
- Brevity is prioritized, but completeness must be preserved.

## Instructions

- Summarize using the five sections below.
- Use markdown with H2 headings.
- Be factual, metric-driven, and concise.
- Avoid filler text or repetition.

## Output Format

```markdown
## Outcomes
[List measurable outcomes delivered this month with metrics]

## OKRs
[Summarize progress against company/product OKRs, including % complete or qualitative status]

## Risks and Blockers
[Highlight key risks, blockers, or trade-offs, with context and mitigation where relevant]

## Next Month’s Priorities
[List top 3–5 priorities with specific deliverables or initiatives]

## Decisions Needed
[Specify clear decisions or approvals required from leadership]
```

## Verbosity

- Keep content succinct (1–2 sentences or bullets per point).
- Prioritize clarity over detail.
- Use metrics or evidence wherever possible.

## Stop Condition

End after presenting the five sections in markdown. Do not include any additional commentary or disclaimers.

## Checklist

1. Role and objective clearly defined.
1. Context framed around leadership needs.
1. Structured into required five headings.
1. Outcomes tied to metrics or KPIs.
1. OKRs mapped with progress.
1. Risks and blockers surfaced.
1. Next priorities listed (3–5).
1. Decisions framed as action items.
1. Output formatted in markdown.
1. Concise, factual, and leadership-ready.

Which **month’s review** should I prepare this document for?
